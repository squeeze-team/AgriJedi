# Grape (Wine) — Crop Intelligence Brief

> **Report Date:** 5 March 2026 | **Outlook Horizon:** Spring–Summer 2026 | **Region:** France (Bordeaux, Champagne, Rhône Valley, Languedoc, Provence)

---

## Executive Summary

The French wine sector is in a **slow recovery phase** after the 2023–24 downturn driven by global oversupply and weak consumption. Grape prices have stabilised around $710–720/mt — up from the $645 trough in April 2024 but still 18% below the 2022 peak. Unlike cereals, grapes have **no futures contract**, making price discovery less transparent and more influenced by regional-quality differentials. The EU vine-pull scheme (2023–24) is beginning to reduce acreage, supporting prices. Weather remains the swing factor: **spring frost risk** (April) and **late-summer drought stress** are the two critical threats for 2026. **Overall signal: Stabilising — structural supply correction underway, but recovery is gradual.**

## Weather Risk Analysis

### Spring Frost Risk (April — Critical for Budburst)

| Year | Apr Mean Temp (°C) | Apr Precip (mm) | Frost Events | Impact |
|------|-------------------|------------------|--------------|--------|
| 2023 | 11.8 | 72.4 | Minimal | Normal budburst |
| 2024 | 12.5 | 68.7 | Minimal | Normal budburst |
| 2025 | 11.4 | 70.5 | Minimal | Normal budburst |

Spring frost has been moderate in the last 3 years, but the **2021 catastrophe** (late April frost destroyed up to 30% of Champagne and Bordeaux buds) remains a realistic tail-risk scenario. Climate models suggest increased frost risk as earlier budburst (driven by warmer winters) exposes vines to late-season cold snaps.

### Summer Heat Stress (Jul–Aug — Berry Development)

| Year | Jul Peak (°C) | Aug Peak (°C) | Jul Precip (mm) | Aug Precip (mm) |
|------|---------------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 2023 | 36.5 | 35.8 | 35.2 | 48.6 |
| 2024 | **37.8** | **36.2** | **28.5** | 32.0 |
| 2025 | **37.2** | **35.5** | 30.5 | 35.8 |

**Impact on grapes:**
- >35°C accelerates sugar accumulation → higher alcohol, lower acidity (Bordeaux, Languedoc most affected)
- Berry sunburn and shrivelling in exposed vineyards
- 2024 was the worst year (37.8°C + only 28.5mm rain in July) — triggered early harvest across Languedoc and Provence
- **Grapes have some heat resilience** vs. cereals: deep root systems and dormancy adaptation help, but quality degradation is the primary risk rather than total yield loss

## Market Structure & Demand Context

### Why Grapes Are Different from Cereals

| Factor | Wheat / Corn | Grapes |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Global trade | Highly traded | Mostly domestic / regional |
| Futures market | CBOT liquid | None — OTC contracts only |
| Demand elasticity | Inelastic (food staple) | Discretionary (wine) |
| Storage | Grain silos (years) | Wine ages but is perishable as grape |
| Price driver | WASDE, weather, FX | Harvest volume, quality, consumer trends |
| EU policy | CAP area payments | Vine-pull subsidies, PDO regulation |

### Demand Trends

- Global wine consumption: −1.0% per year (OIV 2025 report)
- French domestic consumption: −2% per year (demographic shift)
- **Premiumisation offsets volume decline:** average bottle price rising 3–4%/year
- **Export markets (US, UK, China):** weak EUR helps, but tariff uncertainty remains (US 25% tariff threat)
- **Organic / natural wine:** fastest growing segment (+8%/year), commands 15–20% premium
