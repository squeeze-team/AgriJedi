# Corn (Maize) — Crop Intelligence Brief

> **Report Date:** 5 March 2026 | **Outlook Horizon:** Spring–Summer 2026 | **Region:** France (national + key départements)

---

## Executive Summary

French maize enters the 2026 planting season facing a **mixed-to-cautious** outlook. Global corn supply is **tight** (WASDE stocks-to-use at 23.3%, below the 25% alarm threshold), yet CBOT futures have only rallied modestly (+5.6% in 4 weeks) — suggesting the market is partially priced in. The critical risk factor is summer heat: maize peaks in July–August precisely when France has seen 37°C+ peaks three years running. WTI oil surging +20% adds direct cost pressure (ethanol competition, fertiliser, irrigation energy). Domestic yields swung sharply in 2022 (−15% due to drought) and have recovered, but remain vulnerable. **Overall signal: Cautious — tight global supply supports price floor, but production risk is elevated.**

| Year | Jul Peak (°C) | Aug Peak (°C) | Jul Precip (mm) | Aug Precip (mm) |
|------|---------------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 2023 | 36.5 | 35.8 | 35.2 | 48.6 |

**The pattern is clear:** July–August heat stress has become structural in France. Maize's peak growth period (silking, grain fill) coincides exactly with these extremes. **Without expanded irrigation, French maize is increasingly climate-exposed.** 2024 was the worst combination (highest temp + lowest rain).
