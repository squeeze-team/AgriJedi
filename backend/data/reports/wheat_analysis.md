> **Report Date:** 5 March 2026 | **Outlook Horizon:** Spring–Summer 2026 | **Region:** France (national + key départements)

---

## Executive Summary

French wheat is entering the 2026 growing season in a **cautiously optimistic** position. Global supply remains balanced (WASDE stocks-to-use at 31.9%), while CBOT futures have rallied +10% over the past month — signalling renewed speculative interest amid Black Sea logistics uncertainty. Domestic yields have been stable around 6–8 t/ha across major regions, though recurring summer heat stress (3 of the last 3 years exceeded 37°C peak) remains the key downside risk. The weakening EUR (−1.8% over 4 weeks) provides a tailwind for French exports. **Overall signal: Neutral-to-Bullish for spring 2026.**

### Weather Risk Summary (2023–2025)

- **Heat stress months (peak > 35°C):** 6 out of 36 months (Jul+Aug in each of the 3 years)
- **Drought months (precip < 30 mm):** Jul 2024 (28.5 mm) — isolated but critical during grain filling
- **Spring pattern:** Typically adequate rainfall (55–72 mm/month Mar–May), supports wheat through heading
- **Key risk for 2026:** If July–August heat pattern repeats (likely given 3-year trend), wheat will be harvested before peak stress. **Wheat's early-season phenology provides natural resilience to French summer heat.**
